Publication Ethics

Statement of Commitment

Lex Humanitas adheres to internationally recognised publication ethics and maintains a rigorous peer-review process to ensure scholarly integrity, transparency, and academic excellence.

The journal follows the principles of the Committee on Publication Ethics (COPE) and endorses the standards of transparency and best practice jointly articulated by COPE, DOAJ, OASPA, and WAME.

Peer Review Process

The journal employs a double-blind peer-review process, under which the identities of authors and reviewers remain confidential from one another.

Manuscripts are first screened by the Editor-in-Chief for scope and basic structural quality. Submissions passing this initial desk review are assigned to a minimum of two independent expert reviewers. Reviewers assess the paper's originality, methodological rigour, and contribution to existing literature, returning their evaluations to the editorial office with a recommendation to accept, reject, or request revisions.

Duties of Authors

Submitted work must be entirely original; where authors draw upon the work or words of others, these must be properly cited.

Simultaneous submission to more than one journal is unethical and unacceptable. Authorship should be limited to those who have made a significant intellectual contribution to the work, and all sources of funding and potential conflicts of interest must be disclosed.

Duties of Peer Reviewers

Reviewers must maintain the confidentiality of manuscripts under review and evaluate papers objectively with supporting arguments.

Reviewers must decline to review where they feel unqualified or are unable to meet the review timeframe. They must immediately disclose any conflict of interest arising from direct relationships with authors or institutions.

Duties of Editors

The Editor-in-Chief holds full responsibility and authority for the acceptance or rejection of manuscripts, which are evaluated solely on intellectual merit and free of any conflict of interest.

Editors will investigate credible evidence of research misconduct and take appropriate steps, which include issuing corrections, clarifications, expressions of concern, or retractions in accordance with COPE guidance.

Plagiarism Policy

Lex Humanitas has zero tolerance for plagiarism in any form. All submitted manuscripts are screened using plagiarism detection software.

A similarity index below fifteen per cent (15%), calculated after excluding the reference list and properly attributed quotations, is generally regarded as acceptable. Manuscripts exceeding this threshold are returned to authors for revision or rejected outright, depending on severity.

If plagiarism is detected in a published article, it will be retracted immediately, and the authors' institutions will be formally notified.

💡 Service Notice: A plagiarism report service is provided by the journal editorial desk with minimal charges for authors wishing to verify their files.

Corrections and Retractions

Allegations of research misconduct are handled in accordance with COPE's established procedures. Articles found to contain fabricated data, plagiarised content, duplicate publication, or undisclosed conflicts of interest may be retracted.

Retracted articles will remain in the online archive to preserve scholarly history, but will be clearly marked as retracted, with a note explaining the reason. In cases of minor errors, the journal will issue a formal correction or erratum.
