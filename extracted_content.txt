2. About the Journal
Lex Humanitas is a peer-reviewed, open-access academic journal dedicated to publishing high-quality research in law, humanities, social sciences, public policy, governance, and interdisciplinary studies. The journal provides a platform for scholars, researchers, practitioners, and policymakers to disseminate innovative research and critical perspectives on contemporary global challenges.
3. Journal Highlights
The journal operates on a fully open-access basis, providing free and immediate access to all published articles without subscription barriers or paywalls. Scholarly rigour is maintained through a peer-reviewed process, with every submission subjected to double-blind review to ensure objective, unbiased evaluation. As an international journal, it welcomes submissions from researchers across jurisdictions and disciplines, reflecting its commitment to a genuinely global scholarly community. Looking ahead, the journal intends to be DOI-enabled, with Digital Object Identifier assignment planned upon the completion of its indexing milestones. Its editorial conduct is governed by a commitment to ethical publishing, with COPE-aligned publication ethics strictly followed at every stage of the review and publication process. Finally, the journal is structured to ensure rapid publication, supported by a timely and well-managed editorial and review process that respects authors' time without compromising scholarly standards.

Introduction
LEX HUMANITAS: Journal of Law, Society and Humanities is a peer-reviewed, open-access academic journal dedicated to advancing interdisciplinary scholarship at the confluence of law, humanities, social sciences, public policy, and governance. The journal provides a platform for scholars, researchers, practitioners, and policymakers from across the world to disseminate innovative research and critical perspectives on contemporary global challenges.
The journal proceeds from the conviction that law cannot be meaningfully understood, applied, or reformed in isolation from the social, political, cultural, and ethical environments within which it operates. Accordingly, contributions are invited that situate legal inquiry within this broader interdisciplinary context.
Vision
To establish Lex Humanitas as a respected international platform for interdisciplinary scholarship that meaningfully connects legal doctrine with social reality, advancing informed dialogue across jurisdictions and disciplines in the pursuit of justice, equity, and sustainable development.
Mission & Objectives
To publish original, high-quality, peer-reviewed research in law, humanities, social sciences, governance, and public policy.
To foster interdisciplinary inquiry that engages critically with emerging global challenges.
To provide an open-access platform ensuring unrestricted access to scholarship, particularly for researchers in the Global South.
To uphold rigorous standards of academic integrity, editorial independence, and publication ethics consistent with international best practice.
To support early-career researchers and postgraduate scholars alongside established academics.
To serve as a reference resource for courts, policymakers, and institutions engaged in legal and policy reform.

PAGE 5 — PUBLICATION ETHICS
Page URL:  lexhumanitas.in/publication-ethics
Statement of Commitment
Lex Humanitas adheres to internationally recognised publication ethics and maintains a rigorous peer-review process to ensure scholarly integrity, transparency, and academic excellence. The journal follows the principles of the Committee on Publication Ethics (COPE) and endorses the standards of transparency and best practice jointly articulated by COPE, DOAJ, OASPA, and WAME.
Duties of Authors
Submitted work must be entirely original; where authors draw upon the work or words of others, these must be properly cited. Simultaneous submission to more than one journal is unethical and unacceptable. Authorship should be limited to those who have made a significant intellectual contribution to the work, and all sources of funding and potential conflicts of interest must be disclosed.
Duties of Peer Reviewers
Reviewers must maintain the confidentiality of manuscripts under review, decline to review where unqualified or unable to meet the review timeframe, evaluate manuscripts objectively and with supporting argument, and disclose any conflict of interest arising from their relationship with the authors or their institutions.
Duties of Editors
The Editor-in-Chief holds full responsibility and authority for the acceptance or rejection of manuscripts, evaluated solely on intellectual merit and free of any conflict of interest. Editors will investigate credible evidence of research misconduct and issue corrections or retractions in accordance with COPE guidance.
Peer Review Process
The journal employs a double-blind peer-review process, under which the identities of authors and reviewers remain confidential from one another. Manuscripts are first screened by the Editor-in-Chief for scope and basic quality, then assigned to a minimum of two independent expert reviewers. 
Plagiarism Policy
A similarity index below fifteen per cent, calculated after excluding the reference list and properly attributed quotations, is generally regarded as acceptable. Manuscripts exceeding this threshold are returned to authors for revision or rejected outright, depending on severity. Published articles subsequently found to contain plagiarised material will be retracted, and the authors' institutions will be notified. (Plagiarism report Service provided with minimal charges).
Corrections and Retractions
Allegations of research misconduct are handled in accordance with COPE's established procedures. Articles found to contain fabricated data, plagiarised content, duplicate publication, or undisclosed conflicts of interest may be retracted; retracted articles remain in the archive but are clearly marked as such, with the reason stated.

Page URL:  lexhumanitas.in/open-access
Open Access Statement
Lex Humanitas is a fully open-access journal. All published articles are made immediately and permanently available online, free of charge to any reader, without subscription fees or registration requirements. The journal subscribes to the Budapest Open Access Initiative definition of open access.
Copyright and Licensing
Authors retain copyright in their published work. Articles are published under the Creative Commons Attribution 4.0 International Licence (CC BY 4.0), under which readers are free to share and adapt the material for any purpose, including commercially, provided appropriate attribution is given to the original authors and the journal.
Author Rights and Self-Archiving
Authors retain the right to deposit their work in institutional or personal repositories, include it in a thesis or monograph, and use it for teaching purposes, at any stage of publication, provided proper attribution is given to Lex Humanitas.
Publication Fees
The inaugural issue, Volume 1, Issue 1 (2026), is published entirely free of any Article Processing Charge. There is no charge to authors for submission, peer review, editorial processing, or publication of this issue. Any change to this fee position for subsequent issues will be clearly announced on this page and on the homepage well in advance, and authors already accepted under the prevailing no-fee policy will not be charged retrospectively.
