Journal Aim

The Journal aims to provide a rigorous, inclusive, and globally connected platform for interdisciplinary research in law, humanities, and the social sciences. The journal welcomes contributions employing normative, empirical, comparative, and critical methodologies, and encourages scholarship that addresses contemporary legal and social questions in their full political, economic, and ethical dimensions.

Scope — Subject Areas

Lex Humanitas publishes original research articles, review papers, case comments, legislative analyses, and book reviews across the following core academic disciplines and their intersections:

Law and Legal Studies
International Law
Human Rights
Constitutional Law
Criminal Justice
Public Policy
Political Science
Sociology
Humanities
Governance
Law and Society (Interdisciplinary)
Any Other Interdisciplinary Topics

Types of Contributions Accepted

To accommodate diverse forms of scholarship, Lex Humanitas accepts submissions formatted as the following publication categories:

Contribution Type Indicative Word Limit
Original Research Articles 4,000 – 8,000 words (including footnotes)
Review Papers 4,000 – 6,000 words (including footnotes)
Case Comments 2,000 – 4,000 words (including footnotes)
Book Reviews 800 – 1,500 words (including footnotes)

Language Policy

In keeping with its commitment to scholarly inclusivity and public-interest dissemination, Lex Humanitas accepts submissions in two languages:

English

Standard UK or US academic writing style

Hindi (हिंदी)

Formal academic vocabulary in Devanagari script

About Lex Humanitas

A peer-reviewed, open-access academic journal advancing interdisciplinary scholarship at the confluence of law, humanities, social sciences, public policy, and governance.
