Introduction

LEX HUMANITAS: Journal of Law, Society and Humanities is a peer-reviewed, open-access academic journal dedicated to advancing interdisciplinary scholarship at the confluence of law, humanities, social sciences, public policy, and governance. The journal provides a platform for scholars, researchers, practitioners, and policymakers from across the world to disseminate innovative research and critical perspectives on contemporary global challenges.

The journal proceeds from the conviction that law cannot be meaningfully understood, applied, or reformed in isolation from the social, political, cultural, and ethical environments within which it operates. Accordingly, contributions are invited that situate legal inquiry within this broader interdisciplinary context.

Vision

To establish Lex Humanitas as a respected international platform for interdisciplinary scholarship that meaningfully connects legal doctrine with social reality, advancing informed dialogue across jurisdictions and disciplines in the pursuit of justice, equity, and sustainable development.

Mission & Objectives

  • To publish original, high-quality, peer-reviewed research in law, humanities, social sciences, governance, and public policy.
  • To foster interdisciplinary inquiry that engages critically with emerging global challenges.
  • To provide an open-access platform ensuring unrestricted access to scholarship, particularly for researchers in Global South.
  • To uphold rigorous standards of academic integrity, editorial independence, and publication ethics consistent with international best practices.
  • To support early-career researchers and postgraduate scholars alongside established academics.
  • To serve as a reference resource for courts, policymakers, and institutions engaged in legal and policy reform.
