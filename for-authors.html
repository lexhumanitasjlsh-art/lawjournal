Submission Preparation Checklist

Authors are requested to review these guidelines carefully before preparing their manuscripts. Submissions that do not conform to the requirements below will be returned to the authors without peer review, which may delay publication.

Manuscript Formatting Specifications

Element Formatting Requirement
File Format Microsoft Word (.docx) — PDF is not accepted for initial submission
Page Size & Margins A4 Size, 1 inch (2.54 cm) margins on all sides
Font (Body text) Times New Roman, 12 pt, justified alignment
Line Spacing 1.5 line spacing (footnotes: single line spacing, 10 pt)
Title 16 pt, Bold, Centred
Author Name & Affiliation 12 pt Bold / 11 pt Italic, Centred (on the Title page only)
Heading 1 14 pt, Bold, ALL CAPS
Citation Styles Chicago 17th Edition, APA, ILI, or OSCOLA (must be consistent throughout)
Abstract 150–250 words, structured
Keywords 5–8 keywords, comma-separated
Word Limit 4,000 – 8,000 words (Research Article); varies by type (see Aim & Scope)

Manuscript Structure & Organization

▾

A first title page should include the full title, author name(s), institutional affiliation, corresponding author's email address, and a structured abstract of 150 to 250 words, followed by 5 to 8 keywords.

To ensure objective evaluation, the rest of the manuscript must be anonymised, containing no direct author identifiers. The body of the manuscript should generally follow the standard academic sequence:

  1. Introduction (background, problem statement, research questions)
  2. Review of Literature (theoretical framework)
  3. Methodology (where applicable, empirical research design)
  4. Analysis / Discussion (findings, arguments)
  5. Conclusion (implications, summary of contributions)
  6. References (full bibliography list corresponding to citations)
