Privacy Policy

Introduction

Lex Humanitas is committed to protecting the privacy of its authors, peer reviewers, and website visitors. This Privacy Policy details the types of personal data we collect, how it is managed, and the measures we take to ensure its security.

Information We Collect

We collect personal information necessary for the processing, review, and publication of academic manuscripts. This information includes:

  • Author Metadata: Names, email addresses, institutional affiliations, physical addresses, ORCID IDs, and biographical summaries.
  • Peer Reviewer Details: Professional credentials, research interests, and reviewer reports.
  • Web Traffic Data: IP addresses, browser types, and usage patterns collected via cookies and analytical software to optimize website performance.

How We Use Your Data

Personal data collected by Lex Humanitas is used strictly for editorial operations, including:

  • Facilitating double-blind peer review and editorial workflows.
  • Communicating with authors and reviewers regarding submissions.
  • Displaying author names, affiliations, and contact emails on published open-access articles.
  • Sending newsletter alerts or announcements regarding calls for papers (which you can opt-out of at any time).

Data Security & Sharing

We implement industry-standard administrative and technical security measures to protect against data loss or unauthorized access.

We do not sell, trade, or transfer personal data to third parties. However, bibliographic data (such as author names, titles, and abstracts) are shared with indexers, databases (e.g. Google Scholar, DOAJ), and metadata distributors upon article publication to facilitate academic dissemination.

Confidentiality in Peer Review

To maintain the double-blind review process, reviewer reports and reviewer identities are treated with strict confidentiality. Reviewers agree not to disclose details of manuscripts under their evaluation.

Your Rights

Under standard data protection regulations, you have the right to request access to the personal data we hold about you, request corrections to errors, or request the deletion of your personal data from our systems (provided it does not conflict with our administrative requirement to maintain academic records). Contact us at lexhumanitas.jlsh@gmail.com for requests.
